I live in Wellington, New Zealand, and I work as a consultant. I'm originally from Auckland. My parents are from Whangarei. On my mum's side I whakapapa to Te Rarawa and Ngai Takoto in Northland, and on my dad's side I whakapapa to Ngāti Pūkeko in Whakatane and Tainui in King Country.

In my free time I enjoy tennis and Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu*. I play violin and classical guitar after a fashion, and on occasion I've been known to act and M.C.

Previously I studied philosophy, politics, economics and econometrics at Victoria University of Wellington.

What motivates me?

I'm passionate about improving welfare. I’m sympathetic to effective altruism (EA)—roughly, the use of evidence and careful analysis to figure out the best ways of making the world a better place.

Some interests of mine are:

  • Improving the quality of institutional decision-making under uncertainty
  • Getting public- and private-sector actors to think about the long term future
  • Defending human rights
  • Protecting the natural environment
  • Using data science, neural networks, and cognitive technologies to improve policy and project management
  • Education
  • Boosting the rate of sustainable economic growth
